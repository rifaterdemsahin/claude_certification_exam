🏛️ Memory Palace Method

Use a real-world location you know well (your home, commute, gym). Assign each category to a room or area. Place cards as objects in that space.

1
Choose Your Palace

Pick a familiar building with 5 distinct rooms — e.g., your home: Living Room, Kitchen, Office, Bedroom, Bathroom.

2
Map Categories to Rooms

Living Room = Agentic Architecture (red). Kitchen = Tool Design & MCP (blue). Office = Claude Code (green). Bedroom = Prompt Engineering (yellow). Bathroom = Reliability (purple).

3
Place Cards as Objects

Q1 Agentic Loop → a spinning wheel on the sofa. Q28 MCP → a USB-C cable on the kitchen counter. Q46 CLAUDE.md → a constitution scroll on the office desk. Make them bizarre and vivid — the weirder, the more memorable.

4
Walk the Route

Mentally walk through each room daily. See the objects. Recall the card. Say the answer out loud. Speed up as you get faster.

5
Link Related Cards

If two cards are related, place them interacting. The "Coordinator" (Q2) holds a traffic baton pointing at "Sub-agent" (Q8). The "Tool Call" (Q11) menu is cooked by the "MCP Server" (Q34) chef.

🤖 Live AI Study Sessions (Gemini / Claude)

Use an LLM as an interactive tutor. Give it the memory card content and ask it to quiz you, tell stories, or challenge your understanding. Below are ready-to-use prompts.

Prompt 1 — Story Mode Memory Palace

Copy and paste this into Gemini or Claude. Replace [YOUR PALACE] with your real location.

I am studying for the Claude Developer Certification. I have 100 memory cards organized into 5 categories: 1. Agentic Architecture & Orchestration (27%) 2. Tool Design & MCP Integration (18%) 3. Claude Code Configuration & Workflows (20%) 4. Prompt Engineering & Structured Output (20%) 5. Context Management & Reliability (15%) My memory palace is [YOUR PALACE] with 5 rooms. Act as my memory palace guide. Walk me through each room one by one. For each room, ask me to recall the cards placed there. If I get one wrong, tell me the right answer and weave it into a bizarre, vivid story that links it to the room and the next card. Make the story absurd and unforgettable. Start with Room 1.

Prompt 2 — Drill Mode with Weak Cards

Paste this after identifying your weak categories in the app.

I am preparing for the Claude Developer Certification. My weakest categories are: [LIST THEM]. I have a set of memory cards for each concept. Drill me on these weak areas only. For each question: - Ask me the concept - Wait for my answer - If correct: reinforce with a mnemonic and move on - If wrong: explain why, give an analogy, and ask a follow-up twist question Do not move to the next topic until I answer correctly twice in a row. Begin now.

Prompt 3 — Mock Exam Debrief

Use this after taking a mock exam to analyze mistakes.

I just took a practice exam for the Claude Developer Certification and got these questions wrong: [PASTE WRONG QUESTIONS AND YOUR ANSWERS] For each wrong answer: 1. Explain the correct answer conceptually 2. Link it to the relevant memory card mnemonic 3. Tell me what misconception led to my wrong choice 4. Give me a 1-sentence rule to prevent this mistake on the real exam At the end, rank my weak categories and suggest which memory cards to review first.

🎓 Exam Day Protocol

  • Sleep > Cramming. 7-8 hours of sleep improves recall more than an all-nighter.
  • Light breakfast. Avoid heavy carbs that cause a mid-exam crash. Protein + fruit.
  • Review palaces only. 20 minutes before the exam, mentally walk your palace. No new material.
  • Read twice, answer once. The exam may have tricky phrasing. Read the question fully before selecting.
  • Flag and skip. If unsure, mark for review and move on. Return at the end with fresh eyes.
  • Trust the palace. If a question feels familiar, close your eyes, locate the room, see the object. The answer is there.
  • Breathe. 4-7-8 breathing between sections resets focus.
